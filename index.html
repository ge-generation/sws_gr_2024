Background

Origins of immune checkpoints

James Allison and Tasuku Honjo won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 2018 for their discovery of checkpoints.

Role of immune checkpoints

  • Maintain tolerance to self preventing auto-immunity
  • Hanahan & Weinberg identified immune evasion as a hallmark of cancer
  • Immune checkpoints, such as CTLA4 and PD-1, inhibits activation of lymphocytes when there is binding to APCs

Immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs)

  • Monoclonal antibodies against:
    • CTLA4
    • PD-1
    • PD-L1
  • Inhibitory signal
  • Resulting in immune activation & 🤞 restoration of immune recognition of cancer
  • Some ICIs include:
    • Ipilimumab (anti-CTLA4)
    • Pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1)
    • Nivolumab (anti-PD-1)
    • Durvalumab (anti-PD-L1)
    • Atezolizumab (anti-PD-L1)

What is known so far

STK11/LKB1

  • STK11/LKB1 is a serine/threonine kinase involved in:
  • Cellular energy signalling - particularly in stressful situations
  • Tumour suppressor - affects the mTOR pathway
  • LKB1 germline mutation is most famous as the cause of Peutz-Jeghers syndrome
Diagnostic criteria
2 or more histologically confirmed Peutz-Jeghers (PJ) polyps
Any number of PJ polyps in a person with a known family history of PJS in a close relative
Characteristic mucocutaneous pigmentation in an individual who has a family history of PJS in close relative
Any number of PJ polyps in an individual with the characteristic mucocutaneous pigmentation

STK11/LKB1 in lung cancer

  • STK11/LKB1 mutations are found in 30% of lung cancers
  • STK11/LKB1 loss has been associated with:
    • Higher grade, LN metastases and poorer OS in breast cancer
    • Promotes metastases in melanoma tumour models
  • STK11 has been found to be commonly co-mutated with KRAS mutations in lung cancer
  • In lung cancer samples and in vivo studies, STK11 loss lower tumour infiltration of lymphocytes

STING

  • cyclic GMP-AMP synthase (cGAS)-stimulator of interferon genes (STING) pathway is an important immune pathway in cells
  • The pathway leads to formation of pro-inflammatory cytokines after detecting cytosolic DNA
  • This is important in dealing with viral infections, but also in tumours, DNA damage cytosolic DNA

STING haplotypes

  • As explained previously, cGAS-STING pathway has an important role in immune activation
  • However, it is recognised that there are STING SNP variants in people with variable activity:
    • R71H, G230A, R293Q (most common variant)
    • R232H
  • The variants can lead to reduction in STING activity reduced pro-inflammatory cytokines
    • This may have arisen as a protective mechanism in some populations against viral infections
  • However, does activity poor tumour immune response?

Other possible markers

  • TCR repertoire (e.g. diversity)
  • Cytokine levels prior to starting treatment

Why is understanding primary resistance important?

Neoadjuvant treatment

  • Neoadjuvant treatment = Cancer treatment prior to surgery
  • This can lead to:
    • Shrinkage of cancer improved surgery
    • May be only opportunity to have systemic treatment
    • In some cancers, better survival
  • However, not all patients respond to treatment as discussed.

Aims of project

  • Investigate the impact of the STING pathway in immunotherapy resistance
  • Investigate STING haplotypes and their possible role in immune suppressive TME
  • Investigate other possible factors which may impact on immune suppressive TME
